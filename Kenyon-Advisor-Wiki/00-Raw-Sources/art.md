## Studying Studio Art at Kenyon
A faculty of active artists guide students as they discover new media, explore new techniques and perfect their own artistic expressions. Students create art in Horvitz Hall, which offers three floors of state-of-the-art studios and labs — from the light-filled painting studio to the photography and sculpture wings. Senior art students exhibit a small body of their work in the Kahler Gallery.

##  Featured Courses
###  Design Foundations
Explore the principles and sources of design inspiration fundamental to all visual arts, and hone your creativity and imagination through exercises in design analysis, conceptual development and digital technologies.
[ More about ARTS 105 ](https://www.kenyon.edu/academics/departments-and-majors/studio-art/academic-program-requirements/courses-in-studio-art/)
###  Still/Moving: Stop-Motion Animation
Experimental artists and filmmakers use stop-motion animation to create actions that could not be presented through real-time film. Learn how to manipulate materials from paper to found objects, create innovative contexts for movement, integrate live video and sound recording, and experiment with the structure of time while creating your own films.
[ More about ARTS 264 ](https://www.kenyon.edu/academics/departments-and-majors/studio-art/academic-program-requirements/courses-in-studio-art/)
###  Film and Darkroom Photography
Beginning with an emphasis on mastering technical procedures, and evolving into an investigation of the creative possibilities of making images, this course introduces black and white silver-based photography. In the darkroom, experiment with film exposure and processing times, the use of contrast filters, and printing using chemical methods.
[ More about ARTS 222 ](https://www.kenyon.edu/academics/departments-and-majors/studio-art/academic-program-requirements/courses-in-studio-art/)
###  Sculpture II: Materiality and Methodology
The sculpture studios in Horvitz Hall, complete with woodshop, tools and machinery, are home to this exploration of three-dimensional art. Investigate and practice a wide range of sculptural techniques including figure modeling, welding, mold-making and digital fabrication.
[ More about ARTS 310 ](https://www.kenyon.edu/academics/departments-and-majors/studio-art/academic-program-requirements/courses-in-studio-art/)
